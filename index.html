    Handout construction paper, go over what a vision board is, then work on activity

    VISION BOARD ACTIVITY

    In this activity, students will each create their own vision board. A vision board is a collage of words and images that demonstrate what future success looks and feels like for the person completing it. The words and images on the board represent goals and aspirations meant to remind individuals what their future can be if they attain those goals and thus motivate them to do so.

      INSTRUCTIONS
    1. Explain the concept of a vision board to students and how they can create one.
    2. Provide students with examples of vision boards and the time to create their own. Ensure that enough time remains for each student to present their vision board.
    3. After each student has completed their vision boards, allow each student the opportunity to present their vision board. Each student may choose to allow comments from the group. Ask each student to either post their vision board in the classroom for the duration of the program or bring the board home and post it somewhere visible to act as motivation for them to meet their goals. If students post their boards in the classroom, explain that they may bring the boards home with them whenever they choose. Students may edit their vision boards at any point in the future.

    WHAT IS A VISION BOARD AND HOW DOES IT WORK?

    10 POWERFUL VISION BOARD IDEAS

    A vision board is a collection of images, words, quotes, objects, and ideas that help you visualize your goals and achieve your dreams by harnessing the Law of Attraction. It is basically a visual representation of where you hope to be in the future and what you hope to be doing there. By putting your board in a visible location and looking at it daily you focus your positive energy on making the things on your board a reality! BASIC TYPES OF VISION BOARDS Like I mentioned before, the only limit to what you can create is often your own imagination! However, most vision boards fall under these basic categories: Collages (on posterboard, corkboard, or even framed) Digital boards (on your computer, phone, or Pinterest) Bullet journals Dream walls Metal or string grids

    Credit Cards and Loans
    What is a Credit Card?
    1. An account that allows you to purchase goods or services on credit
    meaning you have a certain period to pay the balance off
    wont have to pay off in total, pay in increments every month
    Interest: Cost of borrowing money
    APR: Annual Percentage Rate, includes interest and fees
    Cards may offer 0% Introductory APR, but pay full balance
    Credit Score
    1. Think of it as SATs for adults
    It is a score based on your financial behavior
    Paying dues on time
    length of credit history
    percentage of credit line being used
    How many lines you have opened. mix in lines opened
    banks used score to determine your risk level
    this is way they give you a APR based on your score
    better the score, better the rate, less you pay
    Scores range from 300-850
    i. 300-629: Bad Credit
    ii. 630-689: Fair Credit
    iii 690-719: Good Credit
    iv. 720 and up: Excellent Credit
    d. Check Score for free at annualcreditreport.com
    ii. reported from Equifax, Experian, Transunion
    C. Rules of Credit Cards
    1. Make Every Payment on time!
    2. Payoff balance in full every month
    3. Do not spend over 40% of your limit
    4. Do not close your first ever account, even if not used often
    5. Do not get every credit card offer to you (Do Research)
    6. Check and double-check statement (For identity theft)
    7. Report lost or misused card right away!
    8. Do not withdraw cash from your credit card
    9. Do not charge your card just for rewards
    10. Call ahead of time if you are having trouble paying off your card
    Bank Account

    1. Benefits of Having a Bank Account
    2. Pay bills online (including Credit cards)
    3. Make purchases online
    4. Safer than carrying cash
    5. Direct deposit checks for faster access to funds
